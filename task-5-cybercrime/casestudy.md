## Case Study: UPI / Online Payment Fraud

UPI (Unified Payments Interface) fraud is one of the most common cybercrimes in India, targeting individuals who use digital payment apps like Google Pay, PhonePe, or Paytm. This type of fraud usually occurs through social engineering, where attackers trick users into revealing sensitive information or approving fraudulent transactions.

A typical scenario begins when the victim receives a call, message, or QR code from someone pretending to be a bank official, customer support agent, or even a buyer/seller. The fraudster may claim that money will be credited to the victim’s account and asks them to scan a QR code or click on a payment request link. In reality, scanning the QR code or approving the request results in money being debited from the victim’s account. Sometimes, attackers also ask for OTPs or UPI PINs, gaining complete access to the account.

Students and elderly individuals are often targeted because they may lack awareness about such scams. The consequences can include financial loss, data theft, and emotional stress. In severe cases, victims may lose their entire bank balance.

To prevent such fraud, users should never share their UPI PIN or OTP with anyone, avoid scanning unknown QR codes, and verify the identity of callers before taking any action. Awareness and caution are the most effective defenses against such cybercrimes.
